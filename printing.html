How?

Our process involves embossing your design into a thin layer of chromium metal that sits atop a transparent quartz substrate. The chromium blocks light, creating a permanent, high-contrast black and white image. We achieve a remarkable resolution of 33,866 DPI, which corresponds to a pixel size of just 750nm by 750nm. These photomasks are the same kind used in industrial lithography processes to transfer patterns onto photosensitive materials.

FAQ

Q: What formats do you accept?
A: We can directly accept monochrome PNG, GIF, TIFF, or JPG image files, and PDF, DOC, or TXT text files. We can help you to prepare your content for this process (these are VERY big files!).
Q: Is the text readable?
A: At 8px, the text is only readable under a high-power microscope. You can use bigger font sizes to make the text readable under, say, a high quality loupe or magnifying glass. We can help you pick the best size font for your content and application.
Q: Is the image positive or negative?
A: Any pixel that is ON in your file will be chrome and any pixel that is OFF will be transparent. The chrome is both opaque and reflective, so the image will look positive if front illuminated and negative if rear illuminated. It is kind of like 35mm negative film, except the chrome is mirror reflective rather than matte like film.
Q: What is it made of?
A: The image is etched into an opaque layer of chrome metal (125nm thick) on top of a sheet of quartz glass.
